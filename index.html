PEQUALITY

Gender Equality in PE Class

PEquality is a movement to close the gap between males and females in P.E. class by enforcing a universal standard in P.E. class and California Fitness Tests.

Students should be tested on the same standards regardless of gender.

It is only with collective support that we will be able to change these standards embedded into P.E. and pivot away from these social norms.

It has been universally believed that in terms of physical abilities, males out-perform females. Men on average have 40-60% more upper body muscle and 25-30% more lower-body strength than women.


In society, women are discouraged to appear "bulky." Title IX (Sex Descrimination) for the U.S. Department of Education states that, "

No person in the United States shall, on the basis of sex, be excluded from participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any education program or activity receiving Federal financial assistance."

However, this 1972 Amendment is still not reflected in modern-day P.E. classes and California Fitness Test. Across the state, P.E. teachers modify their requests by gender (i.e. asking boys to run five laps while girls run three). While girls are asked to do 18 curl-ups and 7 push-ups, men are expected to complete 21 curl-ups and 12 push-ups. In terms of flexibility, men only need to reach 8 inches in the sit-and-reach test while women are required to reach at least 10 inches. This perception that men should be stronger while women should be more flexible constricts students to adhere to the long-standing stereotypes.
